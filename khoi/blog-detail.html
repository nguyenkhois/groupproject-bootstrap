Thank You, Fans and Volunteers of the CrossFit Games

Posted on November 10, 2017 by Marcus Lindberg

“Thank you for donating your time … to be a part of something greater, a movement that is bringing the world together, creating communities to inspire everyone to be stronger, healthier, happier.”

No coach likes to see someone kick up to a wall and then collapse.

Before an athlete attempts a handstand push-up, it is imperative that he or she has crossed off all elements of this progression:

  1. Triceps push-up.
  2. Bear-crawl (or pike) handstand push-up on floor.
  3. Scaled handstand with feet on box.
  4. Handstand on wall.
  5. Strict press overhead with barbell.
  6. Strict full-range-of-motion ring dips.

Why go through this short laundry list? Safety and strength development!

Once you have established that an athlete can safely hold a handstand position, he or she can work through the progression that leads to a strict handstand push-up. As with other movements in this series, we recommend athletes perform the strict movement before they kip. The progression of building strength in the strict form is important for several reasons.
